❯ Can you summarize OSR basic rules?                                            

  Thought for 2s (ctrl+o to expand)

⏺ Here's a summary of OSR basic rules, drawing primarily from the B/X
  (Basic/Expert) tradition that most OSR systems derive from:

  ---
  Core Abilities

  Six ability scores, rolled 3d6 in order (no point buy, no assignment):

  ┌──────────────┬────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │   Ability    │                Governs                 │
  ├──────────────┼────────────────────────────────────────┤
  │ Strength     │ Melee attack/damage, forcing doors     │
  ├──────────────┼────────────────────────────────────────┤
  │ Dexterity    │ AC bonus, missile attacks, initiative  │
  ├──────────────┼────────────────────────────────────────┤
  │ Constitution │ HP per hit die                         │
  ├──────────────┼────────────────────────────────────────┤
  │ Intelligence │ Languages, magic-user spells known     │
  ├──────────────┼────────────────────────────────────────┤
  │ Wisdom       │ Cleric spells, save vs. mind effects   │
  ├──────────────┼────────────────────────────────────────┤
  │ Charisma     │ Reaction rolls, max retainers, loyalty │
  └──────────────┴────────────────────────────────────────┘

  Modifiers are flatter than modern editions: 13–15 is +1, 16–17 is +2, 18 is 
  +3. A score of 9–12 gives no modifier.

  ---
  Classes (the classic four)

  - Fighter — d8 HD, any armor/weapon, best attack progression. No special
  abilities beyond hitting things and surviving.
  - Magic-User — d4 HD, no armor, dagger only. One spell at 1st level. Fragile
  early, reality-bending later.
  - Cleric — d6 HD, any armor, blunt weapons only. Turn undead. No spell at 1st
  level (B/X); gets spells at 2nd.
  - Thief — d4 HD, leather only, limited weapons. Skills: pick locks,
  find/remove traps, climb walls, move silently, hide in shadows, pick pockets,
  hear noise. Backstab for double damage.

  Demi-humans (Elf, Dwarf, Halfling) are race-as-class in B/X:
  - Dwarf — d8 HD, fighter with stone-sense, better saves
  - Elf — d6 HD, fighter/magic-user hybrid, immune to ghoul paralysis
  - Halfling — d6 HD, small fighter with bonus to missile attacks, hide
  outdoors, better saves

  ---
  Alignment

  Three-point system: Lawful – Neutral – Chaotic (cosmic allegiance, not
  personality). Law generally = civilization/humans, Chaos = monsters/demons.

  ---
  Core Mechanics

  - Attack rolls — descending AC (lower is better). Unarmored is AC 9, plate +
  shield is AC 2. Roll d20 + bonuses ≥ THAC0 or consult a matrix.
  - Saving throws — five fixed categories (Death/Poison, Wands,
  Paralysis/Petrify, Breath Weapon, Spells), not tied to ability scores. They
  improve with level.
  - Ability checks — roll d20 ≤ ability score (roll-under).
  - XP for GP — 1 XP per gold piece recovered from the dungeon. This is the
  central design engine: treasure is the goal, combat is a failure state.

  ---
  Dungeon & Wilderness Procedures

  The game is built on strict procedures:

  - Dungeon turns (10 minutes): move, search, fight. Torches burn out in 6 turns
  (1 hour). Wandering monster check every 2 turns (1-in-6 chance).
  - Reaction rolls — 2d6: 2 = hostile, 6–8 = uncertain, 12 = friendly. You don't
  have to fight everything.
  - Morale — monsters make a morale check (2d6) after first death or when half
  their side falls. Most will flee.
  - Encumbrance — counted coin-by-coin. 1 coin = 1/10 lb. You move at
  120'/90'/60'/30' per turn depending on load.
  - Wilderness — hex-crawling, getting lost, foraging, hunting. Days instead of
  turns.

  ---
  The OSR Ethos

  The rules matter less than these principles:

  - Rulings, not rules. The referee adjudicates situations; there's no rule for
  everything.
  - Player skill, not character skill. You solve problems by describing what you
  do, not rolling a skill check.
  - Heroism is earned, not assumed. Level 1 characters are fragile (3 HP, one
  hit from death). Survival is an achievement.
  - Combat as war, not sport. Monsters aren't balanced encounters. Running,
  negotiating, or outsmarting is expected.
  - Forget "builds." Death is common, resurrection is rare. Attachment to a
  character comes from what they did at the table, not a backstory.

  ---
  Common OSR systems (all B/X-derived): Old-School Essentials (the gold-standard
  B/X clone), Basic Fantasy RPG (free, modernized), Labyrinth Lord, Swords & 
  Wizardry (0e), Dungeon Crawl Classics (gonzo, funnel, weird dice), Into the 
  Odd (ultra-light), and Mörk Borg (doom metal art-punk).
